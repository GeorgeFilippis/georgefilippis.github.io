About

I am a researcher at RISE Research Institutes of Sweden, part of RIDR and Climate AI Nordics. I apply machine learning and geospatial analysis to environmental problems, with a current focus on decision-support methods for urban climate adaptation.

My work combines GIS, remote sensing, and machine learning to support environmental decision-making. Recent projects span spatial decision-support for urban greening, high-resolution air-quality modelling, and satellite-based monitoring of post-fire vegetation recovery. I am especially interested in methods that make complex spatial trade-offs transparent and defensible for planners and policymakers.

Research

Grey to Green. A spatial decision-support tool that prioritises urban tree planting across a city by scoring where trees are needed against where planting is feasible. It couples multi-criteria decision analysis with LLM- and policy-derived criterion weights, and is validated against expert planner assessments. Piloted in Malmö and Uppsala.

Interests: AI for climate adaptation & urban greening · multi-criteria decision analysis · geospatial machine learning · air-quality and exposure modelling · remote sensing.
